# /design-shotgun

## TL;DR

### Intent

Use this when the team needs to see multiple design directions before choosing one.

### How to use it

Bring it a screen, page, or UI problem and ask for several distinct visual directions rather than one polished answer.

Use it when the right next move is exploration, not critique.

### What is actually useful here

The useful part is not the generation machinery. It is the decision pattern:

- generate several genuinely different directions
- compare them side by side
- collect structured feedback
- choose one direction before refining

That is a better design move than iterating forever on the first thing you saw.

## Core Use

Use this for:

- "Show me options."
- "I do not like how this looks."
- "Explore different directions for this screen."

## Minimal Flow

1. Gather enough context to understand the screen and user.
2. Define a few distinct directions.
3. Generate or describe them clearly enough to compare.
4. Get feedback on what is working and what is not.
5. Choose one direction.
6. Save the choice so later design work has a reference point.

## What It Should Push On

- fake variety that is really the same design three times
- choosing too early
- forgetting what was approved
- letting feedback stay vague instead of directional

## Rules

- Do not generate trivial variations and call them options.
- Do not jump into polish before a direction is chosen.
- Do record what was approved and why.

## In One Sentence

`/design-shotgun` is useful because it creates a disciplined choose-a-direction loop instead of endless visual flailing.
