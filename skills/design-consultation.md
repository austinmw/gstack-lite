# /design-consultation

## TL;DR

### Intent

Use this to propose a coherent design system and visual direction for a product that does not yet have one.

### How to use it

Bring it a product idea, product category, or existing project that needs a clearer visual identity.

Use it when the question is not "is this pixel perfect?" but "what should this product feel like at all?"

### What is actually useful here

The useful parts are:

- synthesize product context into a visual point of view
- separate safe category conventions from deliberate creative risks
- propose a coherent system instead of disconnected design opinions
- turn vague taste into concrete choices about type, color, layout, and motion

That is more useful than just asking for "a design system."

## Core Use

Use this for:

- "Help me create the design system."
- "What should this product look and feel like?"
- "We have no visual direction yet."

## Minimal Flow

1. Understand what the product is and who it is for.
2. Decide whether category research would help.
3. Propose one coherent design direction.
4. Separate safe choices from creative risks.
5. Adjust based on feedback.
6. Write the resulting system down.

## What It Should Push On

- coherence across type, color, spacing, and layout
- whether the product looks generic
- where it should follow the category
- where it should deliberately stand apart

## Rules

- Do not present endless menus of aesthetics without a recommendation.
- Do not hide behind "taste is subjective."
- Do make the creative risks explicit.

## In One Sentence

`/design-consultation` is useful because it turns broad design taste into a coherent system with explicit safe choices and explicit risks.
